About poem

The poem "Choice" is written to critisize the stereotyped arranged marriages
in which a man selects his partner based on her beauty.
In the selection process the character of the woman is given second priority.
The poem also takes references from quotes like 'Don't judge a book by it's cover.'
It talks about how everything gets a careful selection process but the selection of a life partner is based on single criterion called 'fairness'.
In the poem gambling is a metaphor to marriage since people spend too much money on marriage without knowing its outcome.
The selection of a life partner is compared to the 'Beauty Paegant' contest wherein women are judged based on their physical appearance.
