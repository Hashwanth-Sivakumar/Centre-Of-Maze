About poem

This poem is a cry of a rape victim. It is written to show how bad the world has become that the number of child rapes have increased over the past few years. A cry of pain since assault has moved beyond genders and human race.

The first stanza is about joy a new born baby gives to the people around.

The second stanza is about how women in general are judged by the society.

The third stanza talks about the innocence of a child not knowing what people are doing to it when it is being assaulted sexually.

The fourth stanza talks about continuous abuse of children and women even in households as we hear in news everyday.

The fifth stanza talks about what could be the possible reason for someone to think about abuse.

The sixth stanza questions about what changed suddenly.
A baby which was once adored and cherished by all but suddenly why someone sees the child as tool.

The seventh stanza is a call for help to the lord for the pain is unbearable.

The eighth stanza talks about general blaming of cause of rape on the dress of a womam and it ends with a indirect question

"Is a child abused because of its dress ?"
